Credit Card Hack Finder — Simple

    Definitions

    • APR (Annual Percentage Rate): The yearly interest rate charged on your outstanding credit card balance, shown as a percentage. The app converts APR to a monthly rate (APR ÷ 12) to estimate monthly interest.
    • Monthly interest: The interest amount charged for one month based on your current balance and the card's APR. For example, a balance of £800 at 19.9% APR ≈ monthly interest £13.27.
    • Utilization: The percentage of your total credit limit you are using (balance ÷ limit). Lower utilization (commonly under ~30%) is generally better for credit scores.
    • Payment Hacks: Suggested extra payments you could make now. Each shows the new balance and the estimated interest saved this month (and an approximate yearly saving assuming no new charges).
    • Payment history: Your record of on-time or missed payments. Timely payments are one of the strongest factors affecting credit scores.
